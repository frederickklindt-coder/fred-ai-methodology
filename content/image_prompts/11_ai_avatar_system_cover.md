## Cover Image Prompt (Gemini)

```
Generate a vertical social media cover image (1080×1350px).

CONCEPT: A visual showing the "avatar transformation journey" — one person appearing as 
multiple versions of themselves across different scenes.

COMPOSITION:
- Center: Frederick (use attached canonical reference image) — the caricature version, 
  big warm smile, plaid flannel shirt, at his desk with laptop open
- Around him, floating like portals/windows/polaroids at different angles:
  - A version of himself in Paris with the Eiffel Tower
  - A version of himself at a Venice canal dinner at night
  - A version of himself parachuting at sunset
  - A version of himself in a formal/professional setting
- Each "portal" shows the SAME character but in a completely different environment
- The portals have a slight glow at their edges (electric blue #58A6FF)
- His small AI robot companion sits on the desk, pointing at the floating portals

STYLE:
- Semi-realistic caricature matching the attached canonical reference image
- Dark background (#0D1117) to make the portals pop
- Each portal/scene has its own lighting and color temperature
- The center Frederick is warm-lit, grounded at the desk
- Overall feeling: one identity, infinite contexts

TEXT OVERLAY SPACE:
- Leave clean space at the very top (top 20% of image) for title text
- Leave clean space at the very bottom (bottom 10%) for subtitle/branding
- Do NOT render any text on the image — text will be added later

COLOR PALETTE:
- Background: dark blue-black (#0D1117)
- Portal glow: electric blue (#58A6FF)
- Frederick: warm tones (golden hair highlights, warm skin, burgundy undershirt)
- Each portal scene has its own natural colors (Venice = warm amber night, Paris = golden sunset, parachute = orange sunset/blue sky)

MOOD: "One person, everywhere — powered by AI." The image should communicate that this character 
system is real, consistent, and versatile.

DO NOT: Include any text, logos, or watermarks. No stock photo elements. 
No generic AI art. The character must match the attached canonical reference EXACTLY.
```
